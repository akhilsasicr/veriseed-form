Structured expert elicitation for Health Technology Assessment

When an HTA model needs a number nobody has directly measured, VeriSeed collects expert judgment the way it should be collected: structured, calibrated, and blinded, so the result is a defensible input rather than a guess with a false sense of precision.

Built on Cooke's Classical Model and the Sheffield Elicitation Framework (SHELF)

Why not just average everyone's guess?

A simple average treats every expert as equally reliable, which is rarely true and never checked. VeriSeed mixes in a small number of questions with a real, verifiable answer -- invisible to the expert as anything special -- and uses performance on those to work out statistically how much weight each expert's judgment should carry on the questions that genuinely have no known answer yet.

How it works

1
Send the full parameter set, not just the unknowns. Alongside genuinely uncertain quantities, experts also answer a handful of questions with real, verifiable answers -- "seed" questions -- mixed in without being labelled as such.
2
Score each expert against the seeds. Cooke's Classical Model turns performance on the known-answer questions into a calibration and informativeness score per expert -- an objective, statistical measure of how much to trust their judgment on the questions nobody can check.
3
Weight and pool. The genuinely unknown estimates are combined using those performance-based weights, so well-calibrated experts count for more than a method that just averages everyone equally.
